# Understanding Data Privacy

## Overview

This session explored the fundamentals of data privacy, personal data protection, AI-driven surveillance, legal frameworks in India, and the Digital Personal Data Protection Act (DPDPA) 2023.

---

# What is Personal Data?

Personal Data (PD) refers to information relating to an identifiable individual.

Examples:

* Name
* Phone number
* Email address
* Aadhaar number
* IP address

---

# Types of Personal Data

## General Personal Data

Information that identifies a person.

Examples:

* Name
* Address
* Email

---

## Sensitive Personal Data

Information that requires stronger protection.

Examples:

* Biometrics
* Financial data
* Health records
* Government identifiers

---

# Data Classification

Organizations classify data based on sensitivity.

Typical categories:

1. Public
2. Internal
3. Confidential
4. Restricted

---

# Behavioural Data

Data generated through user behavior.

Examples:

* Browsing patterns
* App usage
* Purchase history
* Search history

---

# Digital Data

Information generated during digital interactions.

Examples:

* Device identifiers
* Cookies
* Metadata
* Location information

---

# Legal Framework in India

## IT Act Section 66E

Punishment for violation of privacy.

Covers capturing, publishing, or transmitting private images without consent.

---

## IT Act Section 67A

Punishment for publishing or transmitting sexually explicit material electronically.

---

## IT Act Section 66C

Identity theft involving electronic signatures, passwords, or unique identifiers.

---

## BNS Section 319

Cheating by personation (replaces relevant IPC provisions under Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita).

---

## BNS Section 304

Offences related to extortion and related criminal conduct (verify latest statutory interpretation when applying legally).

---

# Digital Personal Data Protection Act (DPDPA), 2023

India's primary framework governing personal data protection.

---

# Key Definitions

## Data Principal

Individual whose data is processed.

## Data Fiduciary

Entity determining the purpose and means of processing.

## Data Processor

Processes data on behalf of a Data Fiduciary.

---

# Rights of Data Principals

Under DPDPA 2023:

* Right to access information
* Right to correction
* Right to erasure
* Right to grievance redressal
* Right to nominate another person

---

# Obligations of Data Fiduciaries

Organizations must:

* Obtain consent
* Ensure data security
* Notify breaches
* Process data lawfully
* Delete unnecessary data

---

# GDPR: Global Benchmark

The General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) is the European Union's privacy framework.

---

# Seven Principles of GDPR

## 1. Lawfulness, Fairness and Transparency

Processing must be lawful and understandable.

## 2. Purpose Limitation

Collect data only for specified purposes.

## 3. Data Minimization

Collect only necessary data.

## 4. Accuracy

Maintain correct and updated data.

## 5. Storage Limitation

Do not retain data longer than necessary.

## 6. Integrity and Confidentiality

Protect data through appropriate security measures.

## 7. Accountability

Organizations must demonstrate compliance.

---

# Evaluation of Data Privacy in India

### Strengths

* DPDPA 2023 established a national framework.
* Increasing public awareness.
* Growing compliance efforts.

### Challenges

* Rapid AI adoption.
* Cross-border data flows.
* Digital surveillance concerns.
* Limited privacy awareness among users.

---

# Best Practices for Individuals

* Use strong passwords.
* Enable MFA.
* Review app permissions.
* Limit personal information sharing.
* Verify privacy settings.
* Stay alert to phishing attacks.

---

# Key Takeaways

* Personal data is a valuable digital asset.
* AI increases both opportunities and privacy risks.
* Deepfakes represent a growing cybersecurity challenge.
* DPDPA 2023 is India's primary privacy legislation.
* GDPR remains the global benchmark for privacy governance.
* Privacy and cybersecurity must work together to protect users.
