# Cybercrime in the Modern World

One of the major topics discussed during the session was the rapidly evolving cybercrime landscape.

As digital services continue to grow, cybercriminals are constantly finding new ways to exploit individuals and organizations.

## Common Cyber Crimes

### Financial Frauds

Cybercriminals frequently target users through:

* UPI scams
* Banking frauds
* Fake customer support scams
* Investment scams
* Credit card frauds

These attacks are often designed to manipulate victims into voluntarily revealing sensitive information.

---

### Identity Theft

Attackers may steal personal information and use it for:

* Financial fraud
* Account takeover
* Impersonation
* Illegal transactions

---

### Social Engineering

Many attacks succeed not because of technical vulnerabilities but because attackers successfully manipulate human emotions such as:

* Fear
* Urgency
* Trust
* Curiosity

---

### Phishing Attacks

Phishing remains one of the most common attack methods.

Attackers use:

* Fake emails
* Fake websites
* SMS messages
* Social media messages

to steal credentials and personal information.

# Understanding Scams and Fraud Techniques

The session explored several scams currently affecting internet users.

## Common Scam Types

### OTP Scams

Attackers trick victims into sharing One-Time Passwords.

### QR Code Scams

Malicious QR codes redirect victims to fraudulent services.

### Remote Access Scams

Attackers convince users to install remote access software and gain control over their devices.

### Fake Job Scams

Victims are promised employment opportunities and later defrauded.

### Investment Scams

Cybercriminals lure victims with unrealistic financial returns.

### Tech Support Scams

Fraudsters pretend to represent legitimate companies and trick users into providing access to their systems.

# GSR 319(E) and Cyber Regulations

The session briefly introduced **GSR 319(E)**, a cybersecurity notification issued by the Government of India through CERT-In.

The objective is to strengthen cybersecurity incident reporting and improve national cyber resilience.

Organizations may be required to:

* Report cyber incidents
* Preserve system logs
* Support investigations
* Follow cybersecurity compliance requirements

Understanding cyber regulations is becoming increasingly important for cybersecurity professionals.

# Mobile Security Awareness

The session also covered mobile device security.

## Call Forwarding Awareness

The code:

```text
##002#
```

can be used on many networks to disable call forwarding.

This was discussed in the context of cyber fraud awareness because attackers sometimes abuse call forwarding mechanisms during scams.

---

## APK Security Risks

Installing APK files from unknown sources can expose devices to:

* Banking Trojans
* Spyware
* Credential Theft
* Malware Infections

Best practices include:

* Downloading apps from trusted sources
* Reviewing permissions
* Keeping devices updated

# LockBit 3.0 Ransomware

LockBit was discussed as an example of modern ransomware operations.

## Typical Attack Flow

```text
Initial Access
      ↓
Privilege Escalation
      ↓
Persistence
      ↓
Lateral Movement
      ↓
Data Exfiltration
      ↓
Encryption
      ↓
Ransom Demand
```

The discussion highlighted how ransomware groups operate as organized businesses and target organizations worldwide.

# Importance of Cybersecurity Awareness

A major message throughout the session was the importance of spreading cybersecurity awareness.

Dr. Tandon encouraged students to:

* Educate friends and family about cyber threats.
* Help people recognize scams and frauds.
* Promote safe online behavior.
* Contribute positively to the cybersecurity community.

According to the session, awareness is one of the most effective defenses against cybercrime because many attacks succeed due to a lack of knowledge rather than technical weaknesses.
